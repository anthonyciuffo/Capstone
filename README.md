This capstone project explores the relationship between socioeconomic status (SES) and institutional resources in shaping student academic achievement. ðŸ“Š Key Findings

Socioeconomic Dominance: Poverty rates are the single strongest predictor of student graduation outcomes ($R^2 \approx 0.35$).

Institutional Impact: While less powerful than SES, lower student-teacher ratios are statistically associated with higher graduation categories.

The Combined Model: A model combining both SES and Institutional factors explains the most variance ($R^2 \approx 0.48$), supporting the hypothesis that holistic interventions are necessary for equity.
